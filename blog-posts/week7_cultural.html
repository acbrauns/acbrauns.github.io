Cultural Blog: Personal Values

It's hard to choose just one value that I would uphold as the single value that I put above all others. Creativity, close relationships, and introspection are the three most obvious values that I identify with. One thing that in some way encompasses all of those that I think truly is the most important value to me is genuineness.

I to try to live up to my standards for being a genuine person most of the time. I'm a lot more a honest about certain things that many people. The last person to ask me for advice was asking about personal relationships in her life, which is a pretty common theme in our conversations.

When I think about my values, I have mixed feelings about them. I think it is good to be genuine and introspective, have close relationships, and want to create things as a means of self-expression. At the same time, however, some of those things actually do all have the capacity to become extremely self-focused. Introspection can lead to wisdom, or can lead to a twisted self-concept in which I put myself on a pedestal. Creative works can be beautiful, but I can also up shirking the most important people in my life when I'm working on a project that I'm really excited about. Close relationships can be very fulfilling, but they can also lead to unrealistic expectations (i.e. "you should make me happy", particularly in the romantic context). All the things I value the most in life involve an immense depth of feeling, which can be wonderful or very selfish.

This is why I desire to be a more compassionate person. I think I have the capacity to feel things deeply, so if I can do that on someone else's behalf, I would call that empathy. If I can render that when I see someone struggling, it helps me overcome my own perspectives and insecurities. I think that could help to eliminate stereotype threat--albeit, any difficult interaction with other people.

When I'm feeling down at DBC, I think it will help to realize that I'm not the only one struggling. It is going to be a challenge for everyone. Everyone is bringing their doubts and fears to the table. Having enough compassion to realize that I'm not alone will make it easier to have patience for both others and for myself.
